The Ultimate Guide to Kufri Adventures: Horseback and SUV Rides to Mahasu Peak

Nestled just 16 kilometers from the bustling capital city of Shimla, Kufri is a high-altitude haven that transforms into a vibrant adventure hub year-round. Originally a quiet retreat whose name derives from the local word "Kufr" (meaning lake or pond), this picturesque hill station now stands as one of Himachal Pradesh’s most iconic tourist destinations. Located at a breathtaking elevation of 2,720 meters (approx. 8,923 feet) above sea level, Kufri offers a front-row seat to the majestic, snow-capped ranges of the Himalayas.

The undisputed crown jewel of any Kufri itinerary is Mahasu Peak. As the highest point in the region, reaching its summit is a rite of passage for every visitor. But the journey to Mahasu Peak is half the adventure. The mountain trails are rugged, earthy, and perfectly suited for outdoor exploration.

Why Mahasu Peak is a Must-Visit Destination

In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know about exploring Kufri, reaching Mahasu Peak, avoiding common tourist traps, and planning a seamless, unforgettable mountain getaway.

Before diving into how to get there, it is important to understand why Mahasu Peak draws hundreds of thousands of nature lovers, photographers, and thrill-seekers every year.

The Classic Kufri Horseback Ride: A Timeless Mountain Tradition

As the highest vantage point in Kufri, Mahasu Peak offers an uninterrupted, 360-degree panoramic view of the surrounding valleys and mountain ranges. On a clear day, you can even spot the legendary Badrinath and Kedarnath mountain ranges towering in the distance.

The trail leading up to the peak winds through a dense reserve forest rich with towering deodar, spruce, and pine trees. The air here is thinner, crisper, and carries the refreshing scent of the deep woods. Whether you are seeking the pristine white snows of winter or the lush, green refuge of summer, Mahasu Peak is the ultimate escape from the heat and chaos of the plains.

The Adrenaline-Pumping Kufri SUV Ride: Power Meets Comfort

While horseback riding is a timeless classic, it isn't for everyone. The steep mountain trail can be jerky, which may not be suitable for pregnant women, elderly visitors, or anyone with a history of back pain. Furthermore, some thrill-seekers simply prefer the roar of an engine over the clip-clop of hooves. For those looking for a different kind of adventure, our Kufri SUV Ride is the perfect solution.

What to Expect on the Trail

As your horse slowly climbs the winding path, you will be treated to a sensory feast. To your left, dense Himalayan forests; to your right, sprawling valleys that drop away into the clouds. The journey typically takes about 30 to 45 minutes, allowing you plenty of time to soak in the views.

For decades, the most popular way to ascend Mahasu Peak has been on the back of a mountain pony or horse. The trail is approximately 2 to 3 kilometers long, and while it can technically be hiked, the path is shared by hundreds of horses daily. This means the terrain is often muddy, slushy, and steep—making the horseback ride less of a luxury and more of a practical necessity for an enjoyable trip.
